We’re halfway through 2026, and the used superyacht market seems to have reached its highest point already – the incredible sale of Moonrise will be nearly impossible to top before the end of this year. Burgess, the prestigious brokerage firm that represented the seller, wasn’t shy about calling this “the largest brokerage transaction of the year”.

As usual, the exact sale price remains undisclosed, but the last-known asking price says it all: €325 million (more than $370 million), an exorbitant amount that points at the highest, most exclusive (and most contested) end of the market. The megayachts in this niche go beyond the millionaire and billionaire luxury toys that the public is familiar with – they are floating gigantic fortresses wrapped in total privacy.

Usually, when a superyacht hits the market, details and images of its interior become finally available to a wider audience, but that wasn’t the case with Moonrise. This floating behemoth remains as mysterious today as it was six years ago, when it was officially launched. Obviously never chartered, this private mansion on water remains elusive for all us mere mortals, and enjoyed exclusively by its billionaire owners and their guests.

What made this sale even more shocking, beyond the exorbitant price, was how little it took to find a new owner willing to pay that much. Moonrise joined the market right at the beginning of 2026, and was sold less than six months later – an incredibly speedy sale considering the mega price tag. On the other hand, this confirms the high market value of a megayacht like Moonrise.

And as Burgess points out, it also indicates that there’s increased demand right at the tip of the iceberg. In other words, no matter how rough things may be going everywhere else in the world, billionaires will always have an undisturbed appetite for the most outrageous and fascinating luxury toys.

This was the first time that Moonrise changed hands since being delivered to its original owner just six years ago, in 2020. That original owner is said to be none other than tech billionaire Jan Jan Koum, the world-famous founder of WhatsApp, which he sold to Facebook for $19 billion, in 2014. Currently ranking 166th on Forbes’ 2026 Billionaire List, Koum didn’t take baby steps on his way to superyacht ownership.

While other millionaires and even billionaires start out relatively small and gradually evolve to bigger and bigger pleasure craft, Koum went straight for the most stunning megayacht his money could buy. The majestic 99.95-meter (327-foot) Moonrise was delivered by Feadship in 2020, ready to turn heads and win awards.

At the time of its debut, Moonrise was officially the biggest superyacht by waterline length built in the Netherlands, as well as one of the largest superyachts in terms of volume ever built by Feadship. It was soon the effortless winner in the “Displacement Motor Yachts 3,000GT and Above” category at the prestigious World Superyacht Awards in 2021.

The Remi Tessier interior design is still being kept away from the public to this day, but what is known about it is that it centers around an overall minimalist aesthetic, with trendy contemporary features such as ample glazing throughout, high ceilings, and open spaces. A beach club, a helipad, and a spa area are some of the typical superyacht amenities available onboard.

We are only able to able Moonrise’s majestic silhouette from various angles. As Feadship describes it, it has a “modern masculine profile” with a grey hull, a vertical bow that gives it its distinctive elegance, and elongated hull windows. Although hailed for its impressive length at the time of its debut, Moonrise is exceptional when it comes to volume.

Boasting nearly 4,000 GT of volume (thanks to a 15.5-meter/50.8-foot beam), Moonrise can accommodate up to 16 guests across eight superb staterooms, as well as a massive 32-person crew. Performance-wise, it’s fitted with two powerful MTU engines (4,813hp/3589 kW each) capable of top speed of up to 19.5 knots (36 kph).

The top-level steel hull engineered by the world-renowned De Voogt Naval Architects ensures optimal stability and onboard comfort even at maximum speed, while also contributing to the overall fuel efficiency. As for range – more than 8,000 nautical miles (14,816 km) at a cruising speed of 16 knots translate to a world-cruising floating mansion that can go anywhere in full privacy.

Like any self-respecting billionaire’s luxury toy, Moonrise also came with an equally fabulous custom-built support vessel. These special vessels that are also known as shadow yachts are purposely built to accompany mothership megayachts, especially during extended cruises. They are primarily meant to carry additional water toys, equipment, fuel, and supplies for the main yacht, but they are often luxurious pleasure craft in their own right.

Moonrise’s support vessel was the fabulous Nebula, a 68-meter (223-foot) behemoth that also won a prestigious award (the Judges’ Special Award at the 2023 World Superyacht Awards). Nebula was designed by the famous Australian marine engineering firm Incat Crowther and built by the Spanish Astilleros Armon, which operates seven shipyards and a specialist aluminum division.

The owner (reportedly Jan Koum) had specific and challenging requests for Nebula, including a full-size hangar for an Airbus H145 helicopter, as well as generous and comfortable quarters for the crew. This 14.5- by 12-meter (47.5 x 39 feet) heli hangar became an industry-first, the largest on any private vessel at the time. It was purposely built to allow a twin-engine chopper to enter without having to fold its rotor blades, so that it would be ready to go at any time.

The new owner and the new name of the awe-inspiring Moonrise are still unknown, but there’s another Moonrise that has already taken its place. This is the second megayacht reportedly owned by the WhatsApp founder since last year, when it was officially delivered. This Moonrise seems almost identical to the original one, but what’s weird is that despite it being bigger and better, it wasn’t an innovator nor an award-winner.

This 2025 Moonrise is 101-meter-long (330 feet) and it was produced by the exact same team as the original one, including Feadship, De Voogt Naval Architects, and Remi Tessier Design. At first glance, the two vessels look very much alike, but there are important differences.

Unlike the original Moonrise, which proudly displayed five satellite and communications domes on its mast (informally called “Mickey Mouse ears”), the new Moonrise integrates all of its satellite receivers into its superstructure, which changes the look of its exterior profile. It also boasts a higher mast, a longer swim platform, and an owner’s deck.

Upgraded amenities onboard the younger and bigger Moonrise include a bridge-deck cinema, and a bigger sundeck housing a hair salon, a gym, and a massage area. Reduced underwater noise is another perk, resulting from better sound attenuation. All of these enhancements were specifically requested by the owner and it’s clear that the 2025 Moonrise is just as an upgraded version of the original 2020 model, even though it’s ironically much less prestigious.
